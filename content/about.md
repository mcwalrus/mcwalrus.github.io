![Max Collier](/avatar.jpg)

I'm **Max Collier**, a software engineer based in Melbourne, Australia.

Originally I'm from Central Otago, New Zealand, where I spend most of my time
outside with friends whenever I'm back. The situation at home is *generally*
*trying*, and I hope things improve — a full life with dignity feels like it
depends less on your ability to perform at work and more on capital
deployment than it should. I'm looking forward to the day owning a house
**and** having kids is plausible without serious financial risk. If that day
comes, I'd move back.

I spend a large chunk of my time learning across a breadth of technologies.
Software now grows on trees, and it's up to us to pick the fruit that's newly
available. Traditional approaches still apply, but AI has inverted the cost
of R&D in engineering and elsewhere — adopting new skillsets, platforms, and
token-efficient workflows is both an advantage and, I think, critical to
staying competitive.

My skills sit at the Venn diagram of applied Data Science, web-based
Software Engineering, DevOps, and AI. Across my career I've typically worked
across all of these, with geospatial analysis as a recurring thread.
Primarily, I believe it's people, and the good decisions they make, who make
a business. AI is now our best tool for making informed decisions of our
own — and that's what brings real opportunity.

Politically, I sit fairly centre-right with an emphasis on socially minded
policy. [The Opportunity Party](https://www.opportunity.org.nz/) is the one
that best represents me on the spectrum — valuing nature alongside
commercial application, backed by skilled labour within a framework of
cohesive values, aimed at better outcomes for more people. Starboard,
TracPlus, and most recently Entain have each given me skills in areas where I
want to keep having impact — helping businesses take on more ambitious,
creative work.
